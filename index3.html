Samsung About Us

Samsung is a global leader in technology, headquartered in South Korea, and is best known for its diverse range of electronics products, including smartphones, televisions, home appliances, and semiconductors. Founded in 1938 by Lee Byung-chul, Samsung started as a trading company but expanded over the decades into various industries. Today, Samsung Electronics is its most recognized division, playing a pivotal role in the consumer electronics market, especially with its Galaxy series of smartphones and tablets. Samsung has been a key innovator in display technology (AMOLED, QLED), foldable devices, and 5G technologies. The companys mission is to improve the global community through technological innovation and commitment to corporate social responsibility. Its tagline, "Inspire the World, Create the Future," reflects this focus on shaping the next generation of connected technology. 

        
Key highlights about Samsung:

        
        Headquarters: Seoul, South Korea

        Global presence: Over 80 countries

        Major products: Smartphones, TVs, home appliances, memory chips, and display panels

        Innovations: 5G, foldable displays, AI, IoT

        Samsungs business also extends to components and technologies for other companies, particularly in the semiconductor and display industries.
